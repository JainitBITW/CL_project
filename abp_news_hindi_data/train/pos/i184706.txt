In Aurangzeb's reign squared stone and marble gave way to brick or rubble with stucco ornament. Srirangapatna and Lucknow have examples of later Indo-Muslim architecture. He also added his mark to the Lahore Fort and built one the largest mosques in the city, called Badshahi Mosque. He also built one of the thirteen gates, and it was later named after him, Alamgir.
